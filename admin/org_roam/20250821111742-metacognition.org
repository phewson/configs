Questions to provoke critical thinking
https://sheridan.brown.edu/resources/classroom-practices/discussions-seminars/questions-provoke-critical-thinking

* Lower Levels
| Thinking Skills | Purpose                 | Sample Action Prompts                         | Example Questions                                                                                           |
|-----------------|------------------------|----------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Remembering     | memorize & recall facts | recognize, list, describe, identify, retrieve, name | What do we already know about ... ?  \n What are the principles of ... ?  \n How does ... tie in with what we learned before? |
| Understanding   | interpret meaning       | describe, generalize, explain, estimate, predict | Summarize ... or Explain ...  \n What will happen if ... ?  \n What does ... mean?                        |

* Higher Levels
| Thinking Skills | Purpose                        | Sample Action Prompts                       | Example Questions                                                                                           |
|-----------------|--------------------------------|--------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Applying        | apply knowledge to new situations | implement, carry out, use, apply, show, solve, hypothesize | What would happen if ... ?  \n What is a new example of ... ?  \n How could ... be used to ... ?  \n What is the counterargument for ... ? |
| Analyzing       | break down or examine information | compare, organize, deconstruct             | Why is ... important?  \n What is the difference between ... and ... ?  \n What are the implications of ... ?  \n Explain why / explain how?  \n What is ... analogous to?  \n How are ... and ... similar? |
| Evaluating      | judge or decide according to a set of criteria | check, critique, judge, conclude, explain | How does ... affect ... ?  \n Why is ... happening?  \n What is the best ... and why?  \n Do you agree or disagree with the statement ... ? What evidence is there to support your answer?  \n What are the strengths and weakness of ... ?  \n What is the nature of ... ? |
| Creating        | combine elements into a new pattern | design, construct, plan, produce           | What is the solution to the problem of ... ?  \n What do you think causes ... ?  \n What is another way to look at ... ? |

1 From Alison King, “Inquiring Minds Really Do Want to Know: Using Questioning to Teach Critical Thinking,” Teaching of Psychology 22 (1995): 14.
