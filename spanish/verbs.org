A useful rule is that most -ir stem-changing verbs keep the stem change in the gerund, while verbs with a vowel before the ending often take -yendo. For example, leer becomes leyendo and dormir becomes durmiendo.
Handy pattern

    Vowel stem + gerund ending: leer → leyendo, creer → creyendo, oír → oyendo, traer → trayendo.

Stem change preserved: pedir → pidiendo, sentir → sintiendo, servir → sirviendo.

Special cases: ir → yendo and poder → pudiendo.

Easy pattern to remember

    Preterite irregulars usually need memorizing as separate stems, especially tuv-, pud-, pus-, dij-, vin-.

    Future irregulars are more regular-looking: they mostly keep the infinitive minus the final e/i, then add -dr- before the endings.
