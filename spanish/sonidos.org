

* 
- coger -> recoja (subjunctive): g changes to j before a
- traducir -> traduzca (subjunctive): c changes to z before a

Position |	G followed by |	Sound |	Example
------------+----------------------+--------------+-------------
Hard G |	 a, o, u	| Like English "g" in "go"	  | gato, gocho, guapo
Soft G |	 e, i	       |Like "j" (raspy throat sound /χ/) |	gente, girar, gimnasio
J           | any vowel	| Same as soft g	                    | jamón, jira, juego

. -GER / -GIR verbs → g becomes j before a or o
Infinitive	| Yo form (present) |	Subjunctive
coger         | 	cojo	 | coja
proteger  | 	protejo    |	proteja
elegir       | 	elejo        |	eleja

-GAR verbs → g becomes gu before e
Infinitive | 	Yo form   | 	Subjunctive
pagar	    | pago (regular) |	pague
llegar	    | llego (regular) |	llegue

-CAR verbs → c becomes qu before e
Infinitive	| Yo form |	Subjunctive
buscar    |	busco      |	busque
tocar	     | toco        |	toque

-CER / -CIR verbs (after vowel) → c becomes z before a/o
Infinitive	| Yo form (present)  |	Subjunctive
traducir  | 	traduzco	           | traduzca
conocer |	 conozco                  |	conozca


Change |	When |	Purpose
g → j	| before a, o         | Preserve soft g sound
g → gu |	before e	       | preserve hard g sound
c → qu |	before e	      | preserve hard c sound
c → z |	before a, o	| preserve soft c sound

C spelling	Before which vowels?	Sound	English approximation
c	a, o, u	Hard C	"k" in cat, cool
c	e, i	Soft C	"s" in see (Spain/Latin America)
z	any vowel	Z sound	Same as soft c
qu	e, i	Hard QU	"k" in cat
